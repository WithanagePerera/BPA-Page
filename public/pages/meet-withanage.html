Win With Withanage







Bio: Withanage is a junior at Seminole High School in Central Florida. His greatest interests lie in Computer Science, Physics, Calculus, and Chemistry. Outside of the classroom, he plays for his school's swim team in the fall. At home, he enjoys watching anime, working on computer science projects, and working on BPA.



History: Withanage first started BPA in his freshman year when conferences were held virtually. During this time, he was introduced to the role of a leader, gaining a proficiency for it that would continue to grow in the next 2 years. He now leads his team in Video Production and hopes to place 1st.



Beliefs: Withanage believes in the art of cooperation. It's led to success in his personal experiences, and he believes it will make BPA greater. Some of his plans involve cooperating with local chapters at a more extensive level, as well as establishing contacts with other states to exchange ideas and cooperate.





Social Media Questions

What experiences have you had in Business Professionals of America to prepare you for state office?

I’ve been a team leader for 3 years now and with it I’ve gained a lot of experience as a leader. Being a leader in BPA has allowed me to develop strong organization and management skills, which in my experience have been critical to effective leadership.

What experiences have you had outside of BPA to prepare you for state office?

I’m part of several organizations involved in making something or providing some service. During those times, I’m not a team leader, rather a follower. Those experiences have given me perspective on what separates a leader from their followers, the qualities people seek in them, and how to work collaboratively.

What makes you a leader?

From my experiences, I’ve learned how to follow and lead. It comes down to being able to see the bigger picture and visualizing how to get there. For me, I can picture what needs to be accomplished and how to collaborate using my teammates’ strengths to tackle the problem.

Can you commit time to being a state officer?

Of course! I’m passionate about BPA. If it means having the opportunity to make positive change for future delegates and better this organization, then the time I invest is well worth it. It’s my promise to persevere for others because that’s what leaders do.

What changes and enhancements will you make as a state officer?

I want to pursue experiences and opportunities that allow our delegates to work with professionals and businesses and to confidently say they are business professionals. I also want to provide equal opportunities and experiences to chapters across the state through collaboration and targeting constraints with costs, information, and communication.

What do you think your biggest challenge as state officer will be?

I think my biggest challenge would be working with individuals located all across the state on the officer team. With differing schedules and availabilities, it may be a challenge to collaborate and find time to meet, but I’m confident that our shared interests will allow us to overcome this.
